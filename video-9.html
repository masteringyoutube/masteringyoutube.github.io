Managing the “Live Control Room”

GNow it is time to familiarize yourself with the “live control room”.

This “live control room” is, just as its name implies, a control room where you can preview how your live stream is performing.

Consider that the Live Control Room will look differently while you are Live Streaming than while you are not live streaming. The Live Control Room is to be used while you are Live Streaming, so you can manage your Live Streaming properly.

In “stream status”, if you are live streaming, you will see the health status of your broadcast, shown in the form of average bitrate. This data will be in accordance to your bitrate settings. On the right side, you will see the “average live view duration”, “total view time” and “peak concurrent” data.

If your Live Stream is done, or completed. You wont see any data shown in that area.

On the “manage” tab, while you are Live Streaming, you’ll be able to send a Broadcast alert, manage the Slates insertion, manage the Ads insertion, and Create highlights.

For the Broadcast alert, you can show your viewers a “broadcast alert” ranging from “broadcast cancelled” to “we’ll be right back, stand by”. These alerts are useful to use if technical problems arise!

Down the page, you will have a Public View of your Live Streaming. And at your right, you will be able to Open the live chat in a new window.

When you are Not live Streaming, all the manage tab options are disabled.

On the “analytics” tab, you will be able to apply filters, Location, Playback Locations, Camera Angle, and Resolution.

And below it, you will be able to look at Viewer statistics, as well as any error going on.

Down the page you will see the Public View feature once again.

While you are not live streaming, zero results will be shown in that area as well.
