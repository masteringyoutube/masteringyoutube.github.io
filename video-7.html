How to start live streaming the Easy Way

Now that you have your account set up, verified and ready, it is about time to get to the real deal.

As we have told you before, live streaming is relatively easy to get started with, and with the introduction of YouTube’s streaming features it is now easier and more user friendly than ever.

What this means to you is that it will take you no more than a few steps to get yourself started.

In this video, you will learn how to set up your live streams using the fastest and easiest way, which is the “quick events” option.

First, go to your YouTube live dashboard and click on the “live streaming” tab, then click on the “event” option. The page that loads is the section where your live events will be created from start to finish.

Let’s start by clicking on the “new live event” button. This is the button that you will use every time that you want to launch a new live event, so let’s move on.

You’ll be taken to the settings page, specifically to the “basic info” section, where you will be able to configure the basic settings of your live events. First, enter a title and a description for your live event. Then enter a series of tags to help people find your event.

Make sure that the “quick” option is checked under “type”. From now on it is only a matter of clicking on “go live now” to get your event started!

Now, because this is your first time going live, you will need to enable “hangouts” on your browser, which is the software that will allow you to stream video to YouTube, so click on “ok” in the warning window that loads.

A message asking you if you agree to install the hangouts plugin in your browser will appear. Click on “install plugin” to continue.

You might need to install the plugin manually as you would other programs, so proceed as such if needed. Once the plugin is fully enabled, go back to your “events” dashboard.

As you can see, there are three event categories: “live now”, “upcoming” and “completed” events.

“live now” events are events that are already on the air, “upcoming” events are those that are scheduled but have not aired yet and “completed” events are events that are over and archived. As you can see, your event is marked as “upcoming”, and to make it go “live now” you have to click on “start hangouts on air”.

A hangouts window will appear, and you will have to wait for it to fully load until the “start broadcast” button appears and then click on it to start streaming. Once the “live” message appears you can be sure that you are airing! Go back to your “events” dashboard and check on the “live now” option from the dropdown menu on “view”.

As you can see here, your event is now live, and you have some options available to you to configure your event while it is live. You can use the “edit” menu to edit the event settings, the “resume hangout on air” option to resume the stream in case that you stop it or pause it for some reason.

Lastly, you will have access to the “live control room” where you will have full control of the technical aspects of your live events which we will cover in one of our following videos.

Once your event is over, you will simply have to go to your hangouts window and click on “stop broadcast” button and then on the “leave call” button on top to successfully end your event.

Follow us on the next video to learn how to use hangouts appropriately while you are live!
