Managing the “Google Hangouts” Tools

Google hangouts is a communications tool developed by Google that serves as a platform for communicating via instant messaging, video chat and text messages.

It was added to YouTube live as a way to stream video to YouTube without the need for making people configure an encoder to be able to stream simple live events.

In short, Google Hangouts has been implemented into YouTube to make your life easier by letting you stream live events with a few clicks of your mouse.

In our last video we showed you how to install and enable Hangouts on your web browser, and in this video we will show you how to handle the basic Hangouts controls when you are on the air!

So let’s push the “start broadcast” button to ease you into the basics.

It is obvious on first glance that you can have total control over your live event from the hangouts windows without having to go over other control panels outside of it, as you have several buttons and commands on display in the same window where your event is being displayed.

Let’s start by looking at the commands on top.

The commands with the mic and camera symbols will allow you to enable or disable the sound and camera feed to your broadcast with a simple click.

Now let’s click on the “settings” command. There you will be able to select which cameras, microphones, speakers and voice channels to use.

Next to “settings” you’ll have the “adjust bandwidth usage” command to adjust or limit how much bandwidth you allow your stream to use, which will affect video quality but can be helpful when connectivity problems arise.

The last command available here is “invite” which you can use to invite people to your event through Gmail, Google plus or email.

On your left you can enable group chat in the Hangouts window with the “chat” command, to share a screenshot from your desktop with the “screenshare” command and to enable the “cameraman” app in your event to have others’ webcam feeds into your event window.

In the lower right corner you will have a mini window showing you your webcam feed as it is being shown to the event viewers, and below it you will see a command called “links”. When you click on that command you will see two URL addresses.

One address is the “YouTube page” URL which you can use to share your event with others, and the “video embed” URL is an address that you and others can use to embed your event on an external site.

As you can see, managing your live event from your Hangouts windows is pretty straightforward and simple, you just have to familiarize yourself with it to live stream like it was nothing!

Remember that to end your live events you will just have to click on “stop broadcast” and then on “leave call”.

This is how you use Hangouts to live stream the easy way!
