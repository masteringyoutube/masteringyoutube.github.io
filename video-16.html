Promoting your Live Event During the event

In our last video we showed you the ways in which you could get the word about your event out there before it started, all in the name of teasing it to the biggest number of people and, most importantly, getting them hooked until the day of the event.

Now you got all those people showing up for your live stream, and you know that you did a great job, so congratulations! You know that you have proven to yourself that you have what it takes to make this a success. Now go get them!

But wait, is it really enough? You built up momentum and got lots of people to show up for your live stream, but what about the people that weren't mesmerized by your teasers, and what about all the people that didn’t hear of your event, but could be watching it instead of doing whatever they are doing?

You have to act on it, that’s what! The people that are not currently watching your live stream are equally as important as the people that you attracted just by teasing. And the good thing about it is that, by watching you perform, they will get a real taste of what your event is all about!

Think of it as the difference between watching a sports car running and actually getting to drive it. You want as many people inviting others to watch your event as possible, so let’s take a look at some things that you can do to reach them.

Set your event as a trailer for your channel

This is a great customization tool to use to attract people that are not yet subscribed to your channel, the advantage here being that they can see the “now live” icon in the recommended section, and if they access your channel, your live stream will play automatically.

What makes that great as well is that a person is very, very likely to keep watching a video if they know that it is a live broadcast. Even the “now live” icon is usually enough to drive people to watch your content, and if they like it, they will share.

For those already subscribed to your channel, a preview of your live stream will be shown in the “what to watch next” area by the right side of their player.

Create a “real time” trailer for social media

It is very, very important that you create a short, real time trailer for your event minutes before starting your broadcast to upload it to Facebook.

Those not on YouTube will most likely be on Facebook, so make sure that you reach them there as well!

Upload your video with a really short description, make an invitation to participate and share the link to your stream in the comments.

The good thing about this strategy is that people will promote the trailer for you while you are live, so it’s not intrusive and is super effective!

Promote engagement

Read the comments feed and interact with your audience from time to time.

Making a direct mention to a user can prompt sharing, so make sure to engage and entertain your watchers by interacting with them.

That will also help to make them come back for future events!

Update your channel’s appearance

Change your profile picture and your background image to match the theme of your event.

Do this one day before your event goes live.

This will give your event an aura of something fresh and new, something worth sharing!

Remember, the trick here is socialization! if you can offer your subscribers and would be watchers reasons to share your content and bring in more people during the event, you have to entertain them, make them part of the show, making them want to bring more people along!
