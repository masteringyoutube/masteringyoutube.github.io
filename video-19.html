Tips to Improve Live Streaming

There is always going to be room for improvement, and attention to detail can take you to experience success live you have never had before.

In a previous video we told you how important it is to take care of all the small things, and in this video we will help you to build a laundry list of tips to improve your live streaming.

We really recommend you to take notes on this, then make them into a list in the order that best works for you, and stick it to your work space wall.

Write it down, print it or carve it, as long as you do this check list before going live until it becomes second nature to you.

Use a cable connection instead of Wi-Fi:

Your internet connection is as important to your live streams as your talent, because if your live stream fails because of faulty network problems, well, nobody will be able to see you.

Connecting the machine that you are going to use to broadcast your live stream directly to a cable connection instead of Wi-Fi is better because you will skip the many problems often associated with Wi-Fi connections.

Slow signal, interruptions, and lag can be severely alleviated by connecting directly through your broadband cable. If you can’t connect directly, try to get as close to the wireless signal as possible.

Be consistent

Don’t promise your viewers to show them something that you will not. Even something as innocent as teasing something incredible with special effects and dramatic words and delivering a bare bone version of that can feel like betrayal to your viewers, so don’t go over the top.

Being consistent also means to be disciplined. This might not be a job to you, but to your viewers it might well be; think of them as your consumers. Be on time, don’t show yourself in a bad mood and don’t you ever give them less than they have come to expect.

Always test

We might be repeating ourselves here, but it bears weight to be mentioned again.

Test everything before going live.

Test your equipment periodically.

Be organized

Being organized works two mindsets: being organized in your physical space and being organized with your work.

The first mindset refers to being organized with your equipment, your cables, the room or place from where you broadcast, etc.

The second mindset refers to being organized in your goals and in your schedule. You cannot present a great live stream each week if you intend to rush it. Take your time to think of and organize your ideas.

Things can change for the better during the creative process.

Backup your broadcasts

If you start checking around, you will realize that many broadcasters have monetized their live streams by selling them as video packages.

Remember that this is your production, and if you produce something of enough quality and value to your viewers, believe us when we say that they will want to buy it in the long run, especially if they are newcomers to your material!

Center your broadcasts around interaction

We are not talking about simply leaving a comment or a quick message during live chat sessions, we mean real interaction with your viewers.

Promote an occasional giveaway, highlight comments from the chat session, greet people entering the chat, and be prepared to get tough on spammers and disrespectful viewers.

Standing up like that for your fans will help you to grow your audience.

Keep these tips handy and they will become as natural to you as clicking on the “start streaming” button!
