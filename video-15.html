Welcome to Section 4, of our YouTube Live Step-by-Step Video Training.

In this section, we will be showing you about: Promoting your Live Event Before the event, promoting your Live Event During the event, and Promoting your Live Event After the event.

Promoting your Live Event Before the event

Up until now you’ve prepared yourself to deliver your best live streaming as possible. You already know the technicalities involved in producing your live streams to get them ready to launch.

But all that excitement building up around your launch might be preventing you from taking a hard look at what really matters now: your audience.

What if you launch your live stream after hours upon hours of hard work just to realize that you didn’t reach your desired audience? That can be a hard-hitting experience for many, and those unprepared for it can feel like the effort was not worth it after all.

But we want you to succeed, and we want to help you avoid hitting that wall face first, so starting with this video you will learn how to effectively promote your event before it starts, because all things considered, how well your events perform are tied to how well you market them.

Mind you, it’s not about heavy paid advertising. It’s about networking and finding the right people. Having that, the marketing will take care of the rest. So let’s get you up to date with the basics of promoting your event before it launches.

Have social media presence and leverage it

It should come as no surprise to you that social media is one of the most effective ways to promote your event. The power of social media lies in its capacity to drive word of mouth at a fast rate when the delivery and the product promised is good enough.

You already know that what you will deliver is the bomb, and you want other people to know it, so let the people that already follow you do the work for you. The trick here is to start promoting early, so that word of mouth spreads your message through social media.

Now, promoting early will require you to follow this simple formula: depending on how often you launch your events…

-You should tease them seven days in advance, just for one day.

-At three days before the event launches, start sending reminders on a daily basis, preferably in the afternoons.

Create a teaser video

Create a short and interesting teaser trailer for your event. Highlight the important points to be discussed.

Create several short videos to reveal details in a gradual manner. Then promote them on social media and by email newsletter, and follow the seven days promotion guideline.

A good enough teaser will trigger viral sharing. Couple that with early promoting and you’ll have an eager audience!

Timing

Finally, a very important detail that can become a downright obstacle if not taken care of: timing.

If you plan to reach a global audience and not only an audience in a defined geographical area, you need to schedule your events in accordance with each time zone.

The time scheduled to launch your event will never be the same for someone in the United States and for someone in Australia.

Even in the United States time zones can differ to a degree, so plan accordingly!

Doing everything as described to the letter will guarantee you a good audience reach and a successful first launch, so stick with us and learn how to promote your event once you go live!
