What types of Live Streaming YouTube Offers you

YouTube is such a great venue for live streaming thanks to the great and readily available tools that it offers to streamers. Everybody is flocking to the platform in waves to get a piece of the YouTube live streaming cake!

It used to be that live streaming was a complicated process that was best left to the professionals and the people with the money to buy the fancy equipment needed for a broadcast, and then having all of that was not a guarantee that you would be a successful broadcaster, as it happens that reaching millions of people is not an easy task without a little help.

YouTube gets rid of that harsh aspect of the broadcast industry because it already has millions and millions of people consuming content on a daily basis from the platform! Better still, it offers you a variety of ways to live stream, whether from the comfort of your own home, a studio or a stadium!

Let’s learn about each type in this comprehensive yet easy to follow video.

First, start by going to your creator studio dashboard in YouTube. There, click on the “live streaming” tab to see the live streaming options available to you.

Stream Now

Stream now is an innovative and easy way to live stream on YouTube because it automatically detects your bandwidth capacity to give your viewers the best viewing experience possible.

Although you will need to use an encoder to broadcast with “stream now”, the process is greatly simplified as everything will be accessible to you in your live dashboard, and you will not be required to create an event each time that you broadcast.

In short, everything will not only be simplified, but also automated when you broadcast with “stream now”.

Events

“Events” live streaming is now perhaps the most widely used live streaming option by YouTubers, because it offers both simplicity of use and greater control over your live streams.

Basically, you can choose to stream the easy way or the pro way depending on your level of technical expertise and how much control you really want to have over your events.

Let’s take a look at both live streaming methods.

When you want to broadcast using the “events” option, you will see that you will have two types of encoding options at your disposal: “quick” and “custom”. “Quick” is the easy way, and “custom” is the advanced way.

Quick events

When you choose to live stream using the “quick” type of encoding, you will live stream using “hangouts on air” which is software that will allow you to live stream in the same way that you would make a video call.

Essentially, what you are doing is a conference call to your viewers, with all the technical limitations that come with it.

Which shouldn’t be a problem of course, if what you want is to live stream in the easiest and most cost effective way possible. You will have access to basic controls such as camera, chat, voice and sounds, and all of the other technical stuff will be automatically set by YouTube and the hangouts client.

It is even super easy to start live streaming this way. Simply go to “events”, click on “new live event”, enter your event settings and click on “go live now!”

Custom events

When you choose to live stream an event using the “custom” type of encoding, things get a little more interesting. The steps to create a custom event are the same, just that this time you will need to select “custom” and click on “create event”. Once you create your event, you will need to tweak some more settings before actually going live.

You will be able to select a thumbnail for your event, to select basic settings, to add cameras and access a full live control room to take care of the technical performance of your event in real time!

Also, you will need to first set up an encoder to live stream from, which is a bit more complex than streaming from hangouts on air. We recommend you to use custom encoding only if you are sure about what you’re doing.

Those are the 3 types of live streaming that YouTube offers to get the best out of your channel!
